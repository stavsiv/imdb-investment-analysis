🎬 IMDB Data Analysis – Investment Insights
    **Project Overview**

This project analyzes IMDB movie data to provide data-driven investment recommendations for a film industry investor.
The goal is to identify profitable patterns in movie production and guide strategic decisions under budget and risk constraints.

**Business Objective**

An investor is looking to allocate capital in the film industry with:

Limited budget

Preference for Action, Drama, Comedy

Focus on ROI and engagement

Low risk tolerance

Our role: Analyze historical movie data and recommend where to invest.

**Dataset**

The dataset includes:

Movie details (genre, rating, duration)

Financials (budget, gross revenue)

Popularity metrics (Facebook likes)

IMDb scores and reviews

**Tools & Techniques**

Excel (VLOOKUP, Pivot Tables)

Data Cleaning & Transformation

KPI Calculations (ROI, Profit Margin)

Descriptive Statistics

Correlation Analysis

Data Visualization (charts & dashboard)

**Analysis Process**

1. Data Preparation
Merged datasets using VLOOKUP

Created new variables:

Profit = Revenue - Budget

ROI (%Profit)

is_english

duration categories

2. Exploratory Data Analysis

Distribution analysis (histograms)

Genre comparisons (bar charts)

Outlier detection (box plots)

3. Correlation Analysis

Budget vs Profit

IMDb Score vs Revenue

Actor Popularity vs Revenue

4. KPI Analysis

Selected KPI: ROI (Return on Investment)

Reason: Aligns directly with investor goal of maximizing returns under budget constraints.

**Key Insights**

Certain genres (e.g. Action/Drama) show consistently higher ROI

Mid-budget films often outperform high-budget films in ROI

Strong correlation found between actor popularity and revenue

Higher IMDb scores moderately correlate with higher revenue


💡 **Recommendations**

Certain genres consistently outperform others in ROI. After an in-depth analysis, the Horror genre stands out as an excellent investment channel with strong return potential.

Social media popularity should not be treated as a reliable predictor of ROI, as it demonstrates weak correlation with financial performance.

Budget level directly affects both return and risk. Low-budget films can be a good investment but carry high volatility, while mid-budget films maintain strong upside with a far more stable and reasonable risk profile.

**Outputs**

Excel analysis file with full calculations

Visualizations supporting insights
